Key Insights & Recommendations

For Buyers:

  • • SBA financing adds 38 days (59% longer) to closing
  • • Opportunity cost: $482-969/day on typical $1.75M deal
  • • Extra interest over 10 years: ~$529K
  • • Lost cash flow Year 1: ~$56K from delay
  • • Consider if 10% down payment benefit outweighs costs

For Sellers/Brokers:

  • • SBA deals generate 102% higher commissions
  • • Extra $106K median commission gain
  • • Opportunity cost partially offsets gain
  • • Net benefit positive for deals under 15% return
  • • SBA buyers may be more committed (43% usage rate)

Bottom Line:

For deals generating <15% annual returns: SBA financing commission gains exceed opportunity costs for brokers. For buyers: The 10% down payment benefit must be weighed against ~$600K+ in extra interest and opportunity costs over the loan term. The 38-day delay alone costs $19-37K in opportunity value on a typical $1.75M acquisition.
